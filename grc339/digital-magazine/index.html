Beautiful World, Where Are You

Beautiful World, Where Are You follows longtime friends Eileen, an editorial assistant, and Alice, a novelist, grappling with their lives, relationships, contradictions, and cynicism. Emails between the two women — written the way you and your friends hope you sound in your smartest, wittiest, and most introspective moments — give Rooney a venue to explore everything from mental health to politics and climate change to sex. These 29-year-old protagonists confront much of the same anxieties that vex this 29-year-old reader. I expected this novel to make me sad (I have cried during every Sally Rooney book and this one did not break the streak) but what I did not expect was for Beautiful World, Where Are You to soothe the instinct for nihilism with a wild kind of hope. A hope, as the novel offers, “that new things will keep happening, that nothing is over yet.”
